WHY IT’S IMPORTANT TO SELF QUARANTINE DURING THE COVID-19 (CORONAVIRUS) PANDEMIC?
People who have been exposed to the new coronavirus and who are at risk for coming down with COVID-19 might practice self-quarantine.
Health experts recommend that self-quarantine lasts 14 days. Two weeks provides enough time for them to know whether or not they will
become ill and be contagious to other people. You might be asked to practice self-quarantine if you have recently returned from traveling
to a part of the country or the world where COVID-19 is spreading rapidly, or if you have knowingly been exposed to an infected person.
Self-quarantine involves:
■ Using standard hygiene and washing hands frequently
■ Not sharing things like towels and utensils
■ Staying at home
■ Not having visitors
■ Staying at least 6 feet away from other people in your household Once your quarantine period has ended, if you do not have symptoms,
follow your doctor’s instructions on how to return to your normal routine.
